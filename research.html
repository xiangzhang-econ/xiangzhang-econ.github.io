WORKING PAPERS

"Firm-to-firm Relationships, Uncertainty, and Trade"

Awarded the Avinash K. Dixit Prize in International Economics by Princeton University

Abstract: Recent surge in trade and industry policy uncertainties have led to growing disruptions in global value chain relationships, posing additional risks for exporters. I study the effects of relationship termination risk on exporter performance, investment, and search decisions. Event-study analysis highlight that the loss of major international buyers has a long-lasting detrimental impact on exporter performance. Firms losing major buyers see a decline in export value and an increased likelihood of exiting the export market. These firms intensify their search efforts post-loss, yet their newly formed relationships tend to be short-lived. Inspired by these facts, I build an export dynamics model with search friction and endogenous relationship-specific investment. Calibration results indicate that exporting firms do face substantial search costs, as suggested by the past literature. More, the relationship termination risk, and its subsequent influence on firm endogenous choices, are primary factors explaining the observed firm dynamics.
