WORKING PAPERS

"The Dynamic Labor Market Effects of Automation", with Benny Kleinman, Ernest Liu, and Stephen Redding

Abstract: We develop a tractable quantitative model of automation that features two-sided dynamics for both labor mobility and capital accumulation. Workers make dynamic discrete choice decisions over occupations. Capitalists make dynamic investment decisions across occupations. Given data on an economy somewhere along the transition path to an unobserved steady-state, we show how to solve for this unobserved steady-state and the counterfactual transition path in response to automation shocks. We characterize the speed of convergence to steady-state in response to initial conditions at the beginning of our sample period and empirical automation shocks. We show how our framework can be used to predict the future impact of emergent new technologies, such as artificial intelligence.
